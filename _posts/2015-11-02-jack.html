Jack

  • Jack is a Belgian slow-messaging app, which offers a romantic alternative to digital relationships. I looked into innovative and artistic concepts to help them create a beautiful user experience. After research and ideation, I designed a selection of propositions which led to a big re-design of the product and concept itself.

    You can download Jack on the App Store and on Google Play.

  • My first research proposal was to generate colour gradients following the date on which a specific Jack would be available to be read.

  • Each gradient moves clockwise, until it stops to show it's finally available to be read. After waiting for a while, you can finally discover what your friend has been wanting to tell you for a while.

  • Example of the grid principle I designed concerning the proposal of gradient messages, taking the entirety of the screen each time.

  • My second proposal was to use a 3-dimensional space for Jacks: what if you could see a Jack moving towards you as it comes closer to its opening date.

  • The timeline follows the closest and farthest Jacks on the screen: here, you can see that Rachel's Jack will only be available in 2115.
